Shahi Paneer

Description

Shahi paneer is a popular North Indian dish that is rich, creamy, and full of flavor. To make shahi paneer, start by heating some oil in a pan and adding some cumin seeds, bay leaves, and cloves. Next, add some finely chopped onions and sauté them until they turn golden brown. Then, add some ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute or two. Add some tomato puree, along with some salt, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala, and cook until the tomato puree thickens and the oil separates. Add some paneer cubes and cook for a few minutes. Finally, add some cream and kasuri methi and simmer for a couple of minutes. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Ingredients

  • Paneer - 250 grams
  • Oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Cumin seeds - 1 teaspoon
  • Bay leaves - 2
  • Cloves - 2-3
  • Onion - 1 medium-sized (finely chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tablespoon
  • Tomato puree - 1 cup
  • Salt - as per taste
  • Red chili powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Cream - 1/4 cup
  • Kasuri methi - 1 tablespoon (crushed)

Steps

  1. Cut the paneer into small cubes and set aside.
  2. Heat the oil in a pan on medium heat. Once hot, add the cumin seeds, bay leaves, and cloves, and sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the finely chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add the ginger-garlic paste to the pan and cook for a minute or two until the raw smell goes away.
  5. Add the tomato puree to the pan, along with salt, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes or until the tomato puree thickens and the oil separates from the mixture.
  6. Add the paneer cubes to the pan and cook for a few minutes, stirring gently to coat the paneer with the masala.
  7. Pour the cream into the pan and mix well. Simmer for 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  8. Crush the kasuri methi in your palms and add it to the pan. Mix well.
  9. Turn off the heat and let the shahi paneer sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  10. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice. Enjoy!
